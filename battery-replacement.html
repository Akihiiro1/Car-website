Battery Replacement

Automotive batteries can often be a hidden source of problems for drivers, especially in extreme weather conditions. Understanding how batteries function can help you maintain proper operation during the winter and maximize power to all electrical components.

Extreme heat, more so than cold, significantly shortens battery life, which is why many manufacturers are now positioning batteries in the trunk, away from engine heat.

Many drivers mistakenly believe the alternator powers the vehicle's electronics while the engine is running. The alternator simply maintains the battery's charge while driving.

Batteries produce and hold their ability to convert chemical energy into electrical energy. Over time, batteries lose their capacity to store and convert energy efficiently, leading to additional wear and potential failures.

Typically, automotive batteries begin to lose their ability to hold a full charge after about three years. When the battery drop dips too low, it forces the alternator and engine components to work harder, leading to additional wear and potential failures.

Battery Maintenance Guidelines

  • Have your battery checked by a certified technician at fixed points
  • Consider replacing your battery every three years
  • Keep battery terminals clean and free of corrosion
  • Ensure proper connection and mounting
  • Monitor for signs of weakness

Benefits of Regular Battery Maintenance

  • Prevents unexpected failures
  • Extends battery life
  • Protects electrical systems
  • Ensures reliable starting
  • Saves money long-term
