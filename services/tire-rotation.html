Tire Rotation Guide

Proper technique for rotating your tires to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan.

  • Signs you need tire rotation: Uneven tread wear, vibration, poor handling, tire noise.
  • Step-by-step rotation:
    • Loosen lug nuts, lift car with jack, and use jack stands.
    • Move tires to new positions according to your vehicle's pattern.
    • Inspect tires for damage, punctures, or bulges.
    • Check and adjust tire pressure.
    • Tighten lug nuts to manufacturer specs.
  • Safety Tips: Use jack stands, check torque specs, and never work under an unsupported vehicle.
  • Troubleshooting: If vibration persists, check wheel balance and alignment.
  • Benefits: Longer tire life, better safety, improved fuel efficiency.

Pro Tips & Maintenance Reminders

  • Rotate tires every 6,000-8,000 miles.
  • Check alignment and balance regularly.
  • Inspect tires for punctures and bulges.
