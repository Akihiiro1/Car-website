Tire Rotation

Regular tire rotation is essential for maintaining even tire wear and maximizing tire life. Different positions on your vehicle cause tires to wear differently.

Front tires typically experience more wear than rear tires, especially in front-wheel-drive vehicles. The front tires handle most of the steering, braking, and power delivery.

Rotating your tires helps distribute wear evenly across all four tires, ensuring consistent performance and handling. It also helps maintain proper traction and stability.

Most manufacturers recommend rotating tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, though specific intervals may vary. Check your owner's manual for the recommended rotation schedule.

Tire Rotation Guidelines

  • Follow the correct rotation pattern for your vehicle
  • Check tire pressure during rotation
  • Inspect tires for damage or unusual wear
  • Consider wheel balancing with rotation
  • Document rotation service

Benefits of Regular Tire Rotation

  • Extends tire life
  • Improves handling and stability
  • Maintains even tread wear
  • Enhances fuel efficiency
  • Ensures warranty compliance
