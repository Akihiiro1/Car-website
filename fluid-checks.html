Fluid Checks

Regular fluid checks are crucial for maintaining your vehicle's health. Your car relies on various fluids to operate properly, each serving a specific purpose.

Essential fluids include engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, power steering fluid, transmission fluid, and windshield washer fluid. Each has unique properties and maintenance requirements.

Fluid levels should be checked regularly, and the condition of these fluids should be monitored. Dirty or degraded fluids can cause system damage and reduced performance.

Many modern vehicles have electronic sensors to monitor fluid levels, but manual checks are still important for verification and fluid condition assessment.

Fluid Check Guidelines

  • Check fluids when the engine is cold (except transmission fluid)
  • Use the correct type of fluid for each system
  • Look for signs of contamination or degradation
  • Follow manufacturer-recommended change intervals
  • Address leaks promptly

Benefits of Regular Fluid Checks

  • Prevents system damage
  • Maintains optimal performance
  • Identifies problems early
  • Extends component life
  • Reduces repair costs
